Detailed Description

template<LatticeType LR, Compress COMPRESS>
class LatBuilder::StorageTraits< Storage< LR, EmbeddingType::UNILEVEL, COMPRESS > >::Stride

Stride permutation.

Depending on the lattice:

For ordinary lattices: a Stride with parameter \(a \in \mathbb{N}\) is the mapping that maps an index \(i\) to \(a \times i \mod n\).

Consider the unpermuted vector \(\boldsymbol v = (v_0, \dots, v_{n-1})\) for some positive integer \(n\). The \(j\)-th component of the vector with stride length \(a\) has value \(v_{j a \bmod n}\).

For polynomial lattices: A Stride with parameter \(q(z) \in \mathbb{Z}_2[z]\) is the mapping that maps a polynomial \(i(z)\) modulo \(P(z)\) with the polynomial \(h(z) = i(z)q(z) \mod P(z)\) .

Consider the unpermuted vector \(\boldsymbol v = (v_0, \dots, v_{n-1}) \sim (v_{0(z)}, \dots, v_{(n-1)(z)})\) for some positive integer \(n\), where \(j(z) = \sum a_lz^l\) if \(j = \sum a_l2^l\). Let \(P(z)\) be the modulus polynomial. The \(j\)-th component of the vector with stride parameter \(q(z) \in \mathbb{Z}_2[z]\) has value \(v_{j(z) q(z) \bmod P(z)}\).
